Background


I worked within a team to produce hi-fidelity screens for the chatbot creator experience. Due to the fast-paced nature of the start-up, the screens went straight to development, skipping prototyping.

How the cashbot.ai Integration Works


First, a chatbot needs to be created. Any topic works, so in this case, it’s a chatbot that gives dental hygiene advice. Then, the chatbot creator goes to cashbot.ai and sets up an agent, which is an e-commerce store (e.g. Amazon, Shopify) and its product offerings. Then, the creator can train the agent by optimizing the data set and choosing the relevant products. Perhaps, toothpaste is more appealing to crowds than dentures? When the agent is implemented, products are suggested to the chatbot user and every click-through purchase results in profit for the creator and naturally, the e-commerce store.

My Contribution


Given the information architecture, my co-intern and I decided on the visual scheme. To be consistent with the cashbot.ai brand, we took the existing colors and selected just the green to signify the start-up's budding potential and allude to cash.

When it came to displaying the products, I suggested a grid view in addition to the list view. The list view was good for editing captions and titles, but I wanted another view that worked for those who wanted to rapidly choose products and use a default title.

Below are three example screens. Let me know if you'd like to see the rest!

In Retrospect


Looking back, I think the green would fare better as color reserved for action items i.e. buttons. I would remove the green and replace it with grey because it distracts from the product offerings. The product offerings will be a myriad of colors and should rightfully attract attention to themselves, so that the user's eye is focused on the task at hand: filtering products. A year after I look at this project, I feel this experience could be improved with a dashboard design. There are merits to boxing each piece of content, especially when it comes to organization, but it resulted in a lot of pages to walk through. I can foresee this being a pain point for users if they were to return to this experience to edit something.

Cashbot.ai Landing Pages

Website redesign task for two types of customers

Tools
A/B Testing
Sketch
Adobe Illustrator

Background


Cashbot.ai has two target audiences: chatbot creators and businesses.

Chatbots creators hold influence over a base of loyal users with specific interests making for an ideal yet untapped market. Cashbot.ai is the software solution for these creators to tailor native ads for their users and profit from every purchase. On the other hand, businesses looking to acquire customers can referred by these developers and access their engaged audience.

Ideation and Visual Design


My process for the overall feel and look for the pages started with the company's values, as discovered in a branding workshop our company did together. Our CEO pushed for irreverence, which I took as an opportunity to develop a light-hearted, clean website. I created the graphics in Adobe Illustrator. My vision was to bring out the uniqueness of the company through illustrations.

User Testing


I tested the prototypes on various businesses in the Skydeck Accelerator, UC Berkeley’s partner investment fund. I gauged if users could understand the purpose of the website and if the design appealed to them.

In Retrospect


Things I would fix/add:
- Further differentiate the business-facing and consumer-facing styles. - “Did you know?” looks too much like a button, and it isn’t clickable. I would either remove it or restructure the four pieces to fit in a box grid together.
- Animate the illustrations!
- Experiment with font size, weight, and color more.
